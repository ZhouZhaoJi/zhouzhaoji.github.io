Verbs

2018-07-20 18:26:16 | EnglishGrammar

Karen Elizabeth Gordon, author of ‘The Transitive Vampire’, dubbed the verb the ‘heart-throd’ of the sentence. Verbs should do most of the work in a sentence. They describe the state of being of the subject of a sentence, express an action, or describe an occurrence of the subject of a sentence.
Verbs are divided into two classes: finite and non-finite.

Sentences

2018-07-18 14:32:52 | EnglishGrammar

A sentence is the expression of a complete thought. It is made up of a clause. A clause is a group of words that contains a subject and a predicate. Clauses can be put into two kinds: independent/main clauses and dependent/subordinate clauses.
An independent/main clause consists of a subject and a predicate and can stand alone as a sentence.
A dependent/subordinate clause starts with a subordinate word. It can not stand alone as a sentence though it consists of both a subject and a predicate.
Depending upon the number and type of clauses it contains, a sentence may be one of four kinds.
